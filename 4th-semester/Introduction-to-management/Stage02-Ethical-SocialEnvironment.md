# 🎯 Why This Stage Matters

Imagine two companies earn the same profit.

- Company A lies to customers, pollutes rivers, and treats employees badly.
- Company B is honest, protects the environment, and respects its employees.

Which company would you trust?

Most people choose **Company B**.

This is why ethics and social responsibility are important. A successful business should not only make money—it should also do the **right thing**.

---

# 2.1 What is Ethics & Why it Matters in Business

## 💡 Simple Meaning

**Ethics** means knowing the difference between **right and wrong** and choosing to do the right thing.

It is like your **moral compass** that guides your decisions.

---

## 📖 Definition

> **Ethics are moral principles that guide a person's or organization's behavior and decisions.**

---

## Ethics vs Law

Many students confuse these two.

| Ethics | Law |
|---------|-----|
| Based on moral values | Based on government rules |
| Not always written | Officially written |
| Breaking ethics may not be illegal | Breaking law is illegal |
| Guides good behavior | Sets the minimum legal standard |

### Example

Returning a lost wallet.

- Law may not force you to return it.
- Ethics tells you that returning it is the right thing to do.

---

## Why Ethics Matters in Business

Ethical businesses gain something more valuable than profit:

### ✔ Trust

Customers buy from companies they trust.

### ✔ Good Reputation

A company with a good reputation attracts more customers and investors.

### ✔ Loyal Employees

People prefer to work in organizations where they are treated fairly.

### ✔ Long-Term Success

Ethical businesses usually survive longer because they build strong relationships.

---

## What Happens Without Ethics?

Unethical behavior can lead to:

- Fraud
- Corruption
- Customer complaints
- Loss of reputation
- Legal action
- Business failure

---

## Example

Imagine a medicine company knows that one of its medicines has dangerous side effects but still sells it.

The company may earn profit for a short time.

Eventually:

- Customers lose trust.
- Government takes action.
- Company's reputation is destroyed.

Short-term profit can become a long-term loss.

# 2.2 Individual Ethics in Organizations

## 💡 Simple Meaning

An organization becomes ethical only when the people working inside it behave ethically.

Every employee makes decisions every day.

Examples:

- Should I tell the truth?
- Should I misuse company money?
- Should I cheat a customer?

These are **individual ethical decisions**.

---

## What Influences a Person's Ethics?

Many factors shape ethical behavior.

```
Individual Ethics

├── Family
├── Religion
├── Culture
├── Education
├── Personal Values
├── Friends
└── Workplace Culture
```

---

## Personal Values

Values are the beliefs that guide our actions.

Examples:

- Honesty
- Respect
- Responsibility
- Fairness
- Integrity

Managers with strong values usually make better decisions.

---

## Code of Ethics

Many organizations create a **Code of Ethics**.

### Definition

A written document explaining how employees should behave.

It helps employees know:

- What is acceptable.
- What is unacceptable.
- How to deal with ethical problems.

---

## Examples of Ethical Behavior

✔ Being honest with customers

✔ Respecting colleagues

✔ Keeping company information confidential

✔ Reporting corruption

✔ Avoiding discrimination

---

## Examples of Unethical Behavior

❌ Accepting bribes

❌ Lying to customers

❌ Stealing company property

❌ Harassing employees

❌ Sharing confidential information

---

## ⭐ Remember

A good employee follows rules because they are **right**, not because they fear punishment.

# 2.3 Emerging Ethical Issues ⭐

As technology and business change, new ethical challenges appear.

Managers must understand these modern issues.

---

## 1. Technology & Privacy

Today, companies collect customer information.

Examples:

- Phone numbers
- Emails
- Locations
- Payment details

Question:

Should companies misuse this information?

No.

Customer privacy must be protected.

---

## 2. Artificial Intelligence (AI)

AI helps businesses make faster decisions.

However, problems may arise if:

- AI makes unfair decisions.
- AI replaces too many jobs.
- AI uses biased data.

Managers should use AI responsibly.

---

## 3. Social Media

Employees now represent organizations online.

Posting false information or offensive content can damage a company's reputation.

Managers should encourage responsible social media use.

---

## 4. Environmental Issues

Businesses should reduce pollution and waste.

Examples:

- Recycling
- Saving energy
- Reducing plastic use
- Controlling factory emissions

Protecting the environment is now an ethical responsibility.

---

## 5. Workplace Equality

Every employee should receive equal opportunities regardless of:

- Gender
- Religion
- Race
- Disability
- Age

Fair treatment creates a healthy workplace.

---

## 6. Corruption & Bribery

### Bribery

Giving or receiving money or gifts to gain unfair advantages.

Example:

A company pays a government officer illegally to win a contract.

This is unethical and often illegal.

---

## 7. Conflict of Interest

### Meaning

A person uses their position for personal benefit instead of the organization's benefit.

### Example

A manager gives contracts only to their friend's company even when another company is better.

This creates unfairness.

---

## ⭐ Easy Memory Trick

Modern ethical issues usually involve:

```
People

Technology

Environment

Fairness
```

If one of these is harmed, an ethical issue probably exists.

# 2.4 Social Responsibility & Organizations

## 💡 Simple Meaning

Businesses do not exist only to earn profit.

They also have responsibilities toward society.

This is called **Social Responsibility**.

---

## Definition

> **Social Responsibility is the obligation of a business to make decisions that benefit both the organization and society.**

---

## Who Does a Business Affect?

Every organization affects many people.

These people are called **Stakeholders**.

```
Organization

├── Customers
├── Employees
├── Owners
├── Suppliers
├── Government
├── Community
└── Environment
```

Good managers consider all stakeholders before making decisions.

---

## Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

### Meaning

CSR means that a company voluntarily contributes to society beyond making profits.

Think of it as:

```
Business

↓

Earn Profit

+

Help Society
```

---

## Examples of CSR

- Building schools
- Planting trees
- Supporting charities
- Offering scholarships
- Helping during disasters
- Providing safe workplaces
- Reducing pollution

---

## Why CSR is Important

CSR creates benefits for everyone.

### For Society

- Better environment
- Better education
- Better healthcare
- Community development

### For Business

- Better reputation
- Customer trust
- Loyal employees
- Long-term success

---

## Arguments in Favor of Social Responsibility

Supporters believe businesses should:

- Help society
- Protect the environment
- Improve employee welfare
- Solve social problems
- Build long-term trust

---

## Arguments Against Social Responsibility

Some people argue:

- A business should mainly focus on profit.
- CSR activities increase costs.
- Social problems should be solved by governments.

---

## Balanced View ⭐

Modern management believes:

Businesses should earn profit **and** act responsibly.

Profit and social responsibility should work together—not against each other.
