# 1.5 Managerial Skills (Katz's Model) ⭐

## 🎯 Why Learn This?

Imagine you promote the best programmer in a company to become a manager.

Will they automatically become a good manager?

**Not necessarily.**

Being a good manager requires more than technical knowledge. According to **Robert Katz**, every manager needs **three essential skills**.

---

## The Three Managerial Skills

```
Managerial Skills

├── Technical Skills
├── Human Skills
└── Conceptual Skills
```

---

## 1. Technical Skills

### 💡 Simple Meaning

Technical skills are the **ability to perform a specific job**.

These are the practical skills needed in a profession.

### Examples

- Programming
- Accounting
- Graphic Designing
- Teaching
- Machine Operation

### Real-Life Example

A software development manager should understand programming concepts.

A finance manager should understand accounting.

---

## 2. Human Skills

### 💡 Simple Meaning

Human skills are the **ability to work well with people**.

A manager works with employees every day, so communication is extremely important.

Human skills include:

- Communication
- Listening
- Teamwork
- Motivation
- Conflict Resolution
- Leadership

### Example

Two employees have an argument.

A good manager listens to both sides and solves the issue fairly.

That is a human skill.

---

## 3. Conceptual Skills

### 💡 Simple Meaning

Conceptual skills mean **seeing the "big picture."**

Instead of focusing on one department, managers understand how the whole organization works.

### Example

Suppose sales are decreasing.

A top manager doesn't only blame the sales team.

They also think about:

- Marketing
- Product Quality
- Customer Needs
- Competition
- Economy

They connect everything together.

---

## Which Managers Need Which Skills?

Not every manager needs the same level of each skill.

```
Top Managers
⭐⭐⭐ Conceptual
⭐⭐ Human
⭐ Technical

Middle Managers
⭐⭐ Conceptual
⭐⭐⭐ Human
⭐⭐ Technical

First-Line Managers
⭐ Conceptual
⭐⭐ Human
⭐⭐⭐ Technical
```

### Easy Rule

As managers move higher:

- Technical Skills ⬇️ Decrease
- Conceptual Skills ⬆️ Increase
- Human Skills remain important at **every level**

---

## ⭐ Remember

```
Top Managers
Think More

↓

First-Line Managers
Do More
```

# 1.6 Omnipotent vs Symbolic View ⭐

## 🎯 Why Learn This?

When a company succeeds or fails...

Who is responsible?

The manager?

Or are there other factors?

Management experts have two different views.

---

## A. Omnipotent View

### 💡 Simple Meaning

This view says:

> **Managers are responsible for everything.**

If the company succeeds,

the manager deserves the credit.

If the company fails,

the manager deserves the blame.

### Example

A football coach is blamed after losing a tournament.

People believe:

"The coach made poor decisions."

This reflects the **Omnipotent View**.

---

## B. Symbolic View

### 💡 Simple Meaning

This view says:

Managers cannot control everything.

Many outside factors affect business.

Examples:

- Economy
- Government Policies
- Customer Preferences
- Competitors
- Natural Disasters
- Technology

Managers can influence results,

but they cannot control everything.

---

### Example

A restaurant loses customers during a flood.

Was it the manager's fault?

No.

The environment affected the business.

This is the **Symbolic View**.

---

## Comparison

| Omnipotent View | Symbolic View |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Manager controls everything. | Many outside factors influence results. |
| Manager gets all credit or blame. | Success and failure have many causes. |
| Focuses on manager's decisions. | Focuses on environment + manager. |

---

## ⭐ Which View is More Accepted Today?

Modern management mostly supports the **Symbolic View**.

Why?

Because organizations operate in changing environments that managers cannot fully control.

# 1.7 Nature of Managerial Work

## 💡 Simple Meaning

Many people think managers spend the whole day sitting in an office giving orders.

Reality is very different.

Managers perform many activities throughout the day.

---

## Characteristics of Managerial Work

### 1. Managers Work with People

Managers constantly interact with:

- Employees
- Customers
- Suppliers
- Owners
- Government Officials

Management is about **people**, not just paperwork.

---

### 2. Managers Make Decisions

Managers decide:

- What to do
- When to do it
- Who should do it

Every day involves decision-making.

---

### 3. Managers Solve Problems

Problems happen every day.

Examples:

- Customer complaints
- Employee conflicts
- Machine breakdowns
- Budget issues

Managers are expected to solve them quickly.

---

### 4. Managers Perform Many Small Tasks

Managers rarely spend hours on one task.

Their day includes:

- Meetings
- Emails
- Phone calls
- Planning
- Discussions
- Problem-solving

Their work changes frequently.

---

### 5. Managers Work Under Pressure

Managers often face:

- Deadlines
- Competition
- Limited budgets
- Unexpected challenges

Good managers stay calm and make smart decisions.

---

## ⭐ Key Idea

A manager's job is:

```
Plan

↓

Coordinate

↓

Communicate

↓

Decide

↓

Solve Problems

↓

Achieve Goals
```

# 1.8 Workforce Diversity ⭐

## 🎯 Why Learn This?

Every organization consists of different kinds of people.

People differ in:

- Age
- Gender
- Culture
- Language
- Religion
- Education
- Experience
- Skills

These differences are called **Workforce Diversity**.

---

## Definition

> **Workforce Diversity means having employees with different backgrounds, experiences, skills, and personal characteristics working together in an organization.**

---

## Simple Example

Imagine a software company where employees include:

- Fresh graduates
- Experienced engineers
- Men and women
- People from different cities
- Different cultures and languages

This is workforce diversity.

---

## Why is Workforce Diversity Important?

A diverse workforce brings different ideas.

Different ideas lead to:

- Better problem-solving
- More creativity
- Innovation
- Better customer understanding

Organizations become stronger.

---

## Benefits of Workforce Diversity

| Benefit | Explanation |
|----------|-------------|
| Better Ideas | Different perspectives create better solutions. |
| Innovation | New experiences lead to new ideas. |
| Better Decisions | More viewpoints reduce mistakes. |
| Better Customer Service | Diverse employees understand diverse customers. |
| Competitive Advantage | Organizations adapt more easily to change. |

---

## Challenges of Workforce Diversity

Diversity also creates challenges if it is not managed properly.

Examples:

- Communication problems
- Cultural misunderstandings
- Discrimination
- Conflicts
- Resistance to change

---

## How Can Managers Manage Diversity?

Good managers should:

- Treat everyone fairly.
- Respect differences.
- Prevent discrimination.
- Encourage teamwork.
- Give equal opportunities.
- Build an inclusive workplace.

---

## ⭐ Remember

Diversity itself is **not a problem**.

Poor management of diversity is the real problem.

# 🎯 Stage 1 — Final Takeaway

Management is **not about giving orders**.

A manager's real job is to **plan, organize, lead, and control people and resources** to achieve goals effectively and efficiently.

To succeed, managers need:
- The right **skills** (Katz),
- The ability to perform different **roles** (Mintzberg),
- An understanding that **external factors also affect results** (Symbolic View),
- The ability to work in a **diverse workforce**.

These concepts form the **foundation of the entire Management course**. Everything you study in later stages—planning, organizing, leadership, change, and controlling—builds on this foundation.
